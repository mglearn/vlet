SOLO Taxonomy
Structure of Observed Learning Outcome
A practical framework for assessing the quality of student understanding — from surface-level recall to creative abstract application. Approximately 82% of classroom teaching remains at the surface level.
Pre-Structural
Before Formal Instruction
Prior knowledge may be inaccurate. "I don't know what I don't know." Response is irrelevant or misses the point entirely.
Unistructural · Surface
One Relevant Idea
Learner identifies one relevant piece of information but cannot connect it to other concepts.
Multistructural · Surface
Several Ideas, Not Yet Connected
Knows several relevant facts but sees them as separate — cannot yet explain how they interrelate or reconcile contradictions.
Relational · Deep
Understanding Relationships
Links ideas together, sees the bigger picture, explains how parts fit together. Can reconcile conflicting information. Most adults operate here.
Extended Abstract · Transfer
Generalizing to New Contexts
Goes beyond given information, applies principles to novel situations, creates something new. Theorizes and innovates independently.
⚠️

Understanding vs. Production Gap

When a student's verbal understanding is at Extended Abstract but written work appears Unistructural, the barrier may be language or writing skill — not comprehension. Hold a brief conference to diagnose before drawing conclusions.

🤖

AI Integration by SOLO Level

  • Pre / Unistructural: Teacher-led AI for retrieval practice, entry/exit tickets
  • Multistructural: AI-curated viewpoints, SIFT/FLOATER frameworks
  • Relational: AI as brainstorming partner, PRISM support
  • Extended Abstract: AI for rapid prototyping, hypothesis testing

Learning is deeper and more durable when it's effortful. AI should amplify — not shortcut — student thinking.

Structures complex thinking for Deep and Transfer learning phases

S

Consider the broader context and implications

LEARNS Cycle

Mike Bell's instructional design cycle aligned to memory formation

L
Locate

Identify current knowledge and skills

E
Explore

Discover and engage with new information

A
Apply

Put newly acquired knowledge into practice

R
Review

Seek feedback to identify strengths and gaps

N
Nurture

Practice, repeat, and reflect for long-term retention

FLOATER — Critical Thinking

Melanie Trecek-King's framework for evaluating claims and information

F
Falsifiability

Can the claim be disproven?

L
Logic

Does the reasoning make sense?

O
Objectivity

Is it free from bias?

A
Alternative Explanations

Are there other possibilities?

T
Tentative Conclusions

Could this change with new evidence?

E
Evidence

Is there robust, reliable evidence?

R
Replicability

Can results be independently repeated?

RISE Goals

Goal-setting criteria for the EIIR Coaching Cycle's Empower & Engage phase

R
Relevant

Aligns with teacher needs, student objectives, and school priorities

I
Impactful

Leads to significant, measurable improvement in student outcomes

S
Specific

Clearly defined with concrete actions, timelines, and measurable outcomes

E
Energized

Resonates with the teacher's interests and creates genuine motivation

🔍

SIFT — Media Literacy

  • Stop — Pause before sharing or reacting
  • Investigate — Check the source and its credibility
  • Find — Look for multiple angles and perspectives
  • Trace — Find the original source of the claim
By Role
Implementation Ideas
Evidence-based strategies look different depending on your position. Select your role for targeted starting points.
👩‍🏫 Classroom Teacher
🔄 Instructional Coach
🏫 Campus Leader
📋

Plan with ALDO

Use the Amazing Lesson Design Outline to anchor every lesson in a learning phase. Identify the SOLO level you're targeting, then select a strategy with an effect size above 0.4.

🧩

Routinize the Jigsaw

The Jigsaw Method (ES: 0.92) requires multiple uses before it runs smoothly. Start with 3 content chunks. It is the only strategy effective across both Surface and Deep learning phases.

🗣️

Run Reciprocal Teaching Daily

15–30 minutes per day for 15–20 days. Teach one Fab Four role per week: Predict, Question, Clarify, Summarize. Works with video and any media — not just text.

🗺️

Concept Map with BOOM

Brainstorm → Organize → Open connections → Make it meaningful. Use Diagrams.net or CMAP Cloud. Partially completed maps scaffold students who need additional support.

📝

Sample RISE Goal

Jigsaw in Science (ES: 0.92): Create expert groups for 5 key concepts with structured note-taking templates. Target: move students from Multistructural to Relational understanding.

⚠️

Technology Caution

Strategy comes first — then select the tool. Do not use discontinued tools: Jamboard, Flip, or Flipgrid. Current alternatives include Padlet, Whiteboard.fi, Google Slides, and OneNote.

🔄

Use the EIIR Cycle

Structure every coaching relationship: Empower & Engage → Investigate & Innovate → Implement & Improve → Reflect & Realize. Establish RISE goals in the first phase.

📊

SOLO Calibration Sessions

Bring anonymous student work to PLC. Collaboratively identify the SOLO level for each piece. Builds shared, consistent understanding of the framework across a grade level or campus.

📹

Video Microteaching (ES: 0.99)

Guide teachers through a record → reflect → improve cycle. One of the highest effect size approaches for professional learning available. Creates low-stakes, growth-oriented observation culture.

🤝

Co-Plan ALDO Lessons

Focus on Step 3 of ALDO (Select Strategies). Match digital tools to the chosen strategy and learning phase deliberately — not based on availability or trend.

🎯

Sample RISE Goal

Video Microteaching with 5 teachers: Guide each through a recording, collaborative reflection, and one implementation improvement within the coaching cycle.

🔬

Model Strategic Tech Use

During co-teaching, narrate your reasoning aloud: "We're using Diagrams.net because our goal is Deep Learning — it helps students visualize relationships, not just list facts."

🏫

Champion SOLO as Common Language

Reference SOLO levels in walkthroughs, PD sessions, and feedback conversations. Make it part of daily instructional culture — not a one-time professional development topic.

👥

Build Collective Efficacy (ES: 1.01)

Implement monthly data meetings with structured protocols. The highest effect size in Visible Learning research. Create systems where teachers believe in their collective impact on student learning.

📈

Align PD to Effect Sizes

Use effect size data as a filter when evaluating technology purchases and planning professional development. Prioritize tools and training tied to high-impact strategies.

🗂️

Structure PLCs Around Evidence

Shift PLC focus from "what was the score?" to "what does this work tell us about student thinking?" Dedicate recurring time to analyzing student work through the SOLO lens.

🎯

Sample RISE Goal

Campus Argumentation Focus (ES: 0.86): Provide professional development and create a cross-curricular resource bank. Builds critical thinking across disciplines with strong effect.

🔧

Promote ALDO Campus-Wide

Champion ALDO as the shared lesson design framework. Reference it in walkthroughs to ensure technology integration is purposeful and tied to a learning phase — not just present.
